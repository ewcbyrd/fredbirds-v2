About Abbott's Mill Nature Center

Abbott's Mill Nature Center is a beautiful 110-acre nature preserve featuring a historic 1800s grist mill, mill pond, and diverse habitats including wetlands, woodland trails, and meadows. The mill pond and surrounding wetlands provide excellent birding opportunities, particularly for waterfowl, wading birds, and woodland species. The varied habitats support a diverse array of bird species throughout the year, making it a wonderful destination for both beginning and experienced birders.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Trail Access: Well-maintained trails around mill pond and through forest

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Wood Duck, Mallard, Canada Goose
Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, Green Heron
Woodland Birds: Various warblers, woodpeckers, vireos
Other: Belted Kingfisher, Red-winged Blackbird, Tree Swallow
