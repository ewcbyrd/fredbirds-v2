About Cape Henlopen State Park

Cape Henlopen State Park offers exceptional coastal birding with its diverse habitats including coastal dunes, salt marshes, pine forests, and ocean shoreline. Located at the mouth of Delaware Bay, this park is renowned for spectacular hawk migration during fall months and serves as an important stopover point for migrating birds along the Atlantic Flyway. The park's varied ecosystems provide year-round birding opportunities, from shorebirds and seabirds to raptors and woodland species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall migration (September-October) for hawks and warblers
Best Times: Early morning for songbirds, late afternoon for raptors
Hawk Watch: Platform observation area during fall migration

Notable Species

Raptors: Peregrine Falcon, Merlin, Sharp-shinned Hawk, Cooper's Hawk
Shorebirds: Sanderling, Ruddy Turnstone, Dunlin, Red Knot
Migrants: Various warblers, vireos, flycatchers
Coastal: Brown Pelican, various gulls and terns, Northern Gannet
