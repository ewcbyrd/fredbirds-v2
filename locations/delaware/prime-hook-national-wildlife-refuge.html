About Prime Hook National Wildlife Refuge

Prime Hook National Wildlife Refuge encompasses over 10,000 acres of diverse coastal habitats including freshwater impoundments, salt marshes, grasslands, and woodlands along Delaware Bay. This refuge provides critical habitat for waterfowl, wading birds, and numerous other species throughout the year. The extensive trail system and wildlife drives offer excellent birding opportunities, with the freshwater impoundments being particularly productive for waterfowl and the mixed habitats supporting a wide variety of songbirds and raptors.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum bird activity
Trail Access: Multiple trails and wildlife drive provide diverse viewing opportunities

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Wood Duck, Blue-winged Teal, Northern Pintail, Gadwall
Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Green Heron
Raptors: Osprey, Northern Harrier, various hawks
Woodland: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers, Belted Kingfisher
