About DuPont Nature Center

The DuPont Nature Center at Slaughter Beach is a premier destination for observing shorebird migration, particularly during the spectacular spring horseshoe crab spawning season. This unique ecosystem provides critical habitat for thousands of migrating shorebirds, especially Red Knots, which depend on horseshoe crab eggs for fuel during their epic journey from South America to Arctic breeding grounds. The center offers excellent educational programs and viewing opportunities for this remarkable natural phenomenon.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration during horseshoe crab spawning (May-June)
Best Times: High tide periods for optimal shorebird viewing
Special Events: Red Knot migration coincides with horseshoe crab spawning

Notable Species

Shorebirds: Red Knot, Ruddy Turnstone, Sanderling, Dunlin
Gulls & Terns: Laughing Gull, various tern species
Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Snowy Egret
Other: Osprey, Brown Pelican, various migrant songbirds
