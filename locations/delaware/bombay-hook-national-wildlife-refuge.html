About Bombay Hook National Wildlife Refuge

Bombay Hook National Wildlife Refuge is a premier waterfowl habitat featuring extensive salt marshes and freshwater impoundments. The refuge offers an excellent auto tour route that provides access to diverse wetland habitats. This location is renowned for its spectacular fall and winter waterfowl migration, including massive flocks of Snow Geese and Tundra Swans. The refuge's varied habitats support over 300 bird species throughout the year, making it one of the most important birding destinations on the Atlantic Flyway.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall and winter waterfowl migration (October through February)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Notable Events: Snow Goose migration peaks in November and December

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Snow Goose, Tundra Swan, Canvasback, Redhead, Northern Pintail
Raptors: Bald Eagle, Northern Harrier, Peregrine Falcon
Shorebirds: Dunlin, Short-billed Dowitcher, Greater Yellowlegs
Other: Saltmarsh Sparrow, Marsh Wren, Great Blue Heron
