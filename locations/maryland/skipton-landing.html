About Skipton Landing

Skipton Landing provides excellent waterfront access to the tidal creeks and marshes of Dorchester County, offering outstanding opportunities for observing waterbirds and marsh species. This location features expansive views of tidal flats and creek systems that attract a diverse array of wading birds, waterfowl, and shorebirds. The area serves as an excellent complement to nearby Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge, providing access to different wetland habitats and tidal environments.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning and around changing tides
Tidal Access: Excellent viewing of tidal creek and marsh systems

Notable Species

Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Snowy Egret, Green Heron
Waterfowl: Various ducks, Canada Goose, Mute Swan
Raptors: Osprey, Northern Harrier, Bald Eagle
Marsh Birds: Marsh Wren, Red-winged Blackbird, Clapper Rail

Location

Skipton Landing
