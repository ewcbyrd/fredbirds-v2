About Point Lookout State Park

Point Lookout State Park is strategically located at the confluence of the Potomac River and Chesapeake Bay, making it an exceptional location for birding and historical exploration. This 1,042-acre park features diverse habitats including beaches, marshes, and woodlands, while its unique geographic position creates a natural funnel for migrating birds. The park is particularly renowned for excellent hawk migration viewing and offers year-round birding opportunities in a historically significant setting.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall migration and winter (September-February)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Hawk Migration: September-October for peak raptor movement

Notable Species

Raptors: Various hawks during migration, Bald Eagle year-round
Waterfowl: Various ducks, geese, Tundra Swan in winter
Migrants: Warblers, vireos, flycatchers during spring and fall
Year-round: Brown Pelican, Osprey, Great Blue Heron
