About Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge

Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge is one of the premier waterfowl destinations on the East Coast, encompassing over 28,000 acres of tidal salt marshes, freshwater impoundments, meadows, and forests. The refuge is famous for its spectacular winter waterfowl concentrations, including thousands of Tundra Swans, Canada Geese, and various duck species. The Wildlife Drive provides excellent access to diverse habitats, while the refuge also serves as important habitat for the endangered Delmarva Peninsula Fox Squirrel.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Winter waterfowl season (December-February)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Wildlife Drive: 4-mile auto tour provides excellent viewing opportunities

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Tundra Swan, Canada Goose, Canvasback, Redhead, various ducks
Raptors: Bald Eagle, Northern Harrier, Rough-legged Hawk
Other: Great Blue Heron, Osprey, Brown-headed Nuthatch
Special: Delmarva Peninsula Fox Squirrel habitat
