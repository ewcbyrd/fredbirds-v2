About Longwood Road

Longwood Road in Dorchester County is renowned as one of the premier winter raptor viewing locations on the East Coast. This agricultural area features vast open fields and wetlands that attract an exceptional concentration of wintering raptors, including the rare Short-eared Owl and Rough-legged Hawk. The road provides excellent roadside birding opportunities, allowing birders to systematically scan the fields and marshes for hunting raptors from the comfort of their vehicles.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Winter (December-February)
Best Times: Late afternoon for Short-eared Owls, midday for hawks
Viewing Style: Roadside birding from vehicle or pulloffs

Notable Species

Winter Raptors: Short-eared Owl, Rough-legged Hawk, Northern Harrier
Year-round Raptors: Red-tailed Hawk, American Kestrel
Other Winter Species: Snow Bunting, Lapland Longspur, Horned Lark
Occasional: Snowy Owl, Peregrine Falcon

Location

Longwood Road
Dorchester County, Maryland
(Various access points along rural agricultural roads)

Note: Best accessed as part of organized birding trips due to multiple scattered viewing locations
