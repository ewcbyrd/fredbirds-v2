About Patuxent Research Refuge

Patuxent Research Refuge is the nation's only National Wildlife Refuge established specifically to support wildlife research. Encompassing over 12,800 acres, the refuge features diverse habitats including forests, meadows, wetlands, and streams that support an impressive variety of bird species. The refuge's research mission has led to important conservation breakthroughs, while its trails and visitor center provide excellent opportunities for birding and environmental education.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum bird activity
Trail System: Multiple trails through diverse habitats

Notable Species

Woodland Birds: Barred Owl, Pileated Woodpecker, various warblers
Wetland Species: Wood Duck, Great Blue Heron, Belted Kingfisher
Grassland Birds: Eastern Bluebird, Indigo Bunting, Wild Turkey
Research Focus: Breeding bird populations and migration patterns
