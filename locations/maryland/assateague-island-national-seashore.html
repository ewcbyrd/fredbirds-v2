About Assateague Island National Seashore

Assateague Island National Seashore is a pristine 37-mile barrier island famous for its wild ponies and exceptional birding opportunities. The island's diverse habitats include ocean beaches, salt marshes, maritime forests, and freshwater impoundments, supporting an incredible variety of bird species throughout the year. This location is particularly renowned for its shorebird populations, nesting Piping Plovers, and as a critical stopover point for migrating birds along the Atlantic Flyway.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and fall migration (April-May, September-October)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum bird activity
Special Features: Nesting shorebirds in summer, waterfowl in winter

Notable Species

Shorebirds: Piping Plover, Least Tern, American Oystercatcher, various sandpipers
Raptors: Northern Harrier, Peregrine Falcon, Short-eared Owl
Waterfowl: Various ducks, geese, and swans in winter
Other: Brown Pelican, Osprey, Saltmarsh Sparrow, various warblers
