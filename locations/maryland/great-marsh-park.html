About Great Marsh Park

Great Marsh Park is a 70-acre waterfront park in Cambridge featuring excellent tidal marsh habitat along the Choptank River. The park's elevated boardwalk provides outstanding access to marsh birds and waterbirds while offering scenic views of the expansive tidal wetlands. This location is particularly productive for observing the daily rhythm of tidal marsh birds and serves as an excellent complement to nearby Blackwater National Wildlife Refuge.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and summer breeding season (April-August)
Best Times: Early morning and around high tide
Boardwalk Access: Elevated viewing platform over tidal marsh

Notable Species

Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Snowy Egret, Green Heron
Marsh Birds: Marsh Wren, Red-winged Blackbird, Saltmarsh Sparrow
Raptors: Osprey, Northern Harrier
Other: Belted Kingfisher, various swallows, Clapper Rail
