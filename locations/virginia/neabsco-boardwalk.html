About Neabsco Boardwalk

The Neabsco Boardwalk provides exceptional access to tidal wetlands along Neabsco Creek, offering prime birding opportunities in one of the most productive marsh systems in the region. This elevated boardwalk extends into the heart of the marsh, allowing close observation of waterbirds and marsh species while minimizing disturbance to these sensitive habitats. The strategic location where Neabsco Creek meets the Potomac River creates ideal conditions for both resident and migrating species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning and evening at changing tides
Boardwalk: Elevated access minimizes disturbance to sensitive marsh

Notable Species

Waterbirds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Green Heron
Raptors: Osprey, Bald Eagle, Northern Harrier
Waterfowl: Various ducks, Pied-billed Grebe
Marsh Species: Marsh Wren, Red-winged Blackbird, Common Yellowthroat
