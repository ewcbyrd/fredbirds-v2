About CBBT Island #4

Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel Island #4 is one of the most unique birding locations on the East Coast, offering unparalleled pelagic birding opportunities from a man-made island in the middle of Chesapeake Bay. This artificial island provides the rare opportunity to observe offshore seabirds and marine life from a stable platform, making it accessible to birders who might not otherwise experience pelagic birding. The location is famous for attracting rare seabirds and providing excellent views of gannets, loons, and other offshore species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall and winter for seabirds (October-March)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Access: Drive to Island #4 via Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel (toll required)

Notable Species

Seabirds: Northern Gannet, Common Loon, Red-throated Loon
Gulls: Various gull species, occasional rare gulls
Terns: Royal Tern, Sandwich Tern, Forster's Tern
Rarities: Occasional rare seabirds and offshore species

Location

Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel
Island #4
Virginia Beach, VA

Access: Drive via US Route 13 Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel
Important: Visit must be arranged in advance with CBBT Administration for a fixed date/time
Note: Bridge-tunnel toll required; fishing area with restaurant facilities
