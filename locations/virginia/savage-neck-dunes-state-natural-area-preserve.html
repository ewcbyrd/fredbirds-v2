About Savage Neck Dunes State Natural Area Preserve

Savage Neck Dunes State Natural Area Preserve protects 298 acres of rare coastal dune system and maritime forest on Virginia's Eastern Shore. This unique preserve features one of the few remaining intact dune systems in the Chesapeake Bay region, supporting specialized plant and animal communities adapted to the harsh coastal environment. The preserve's diverse habitats include primary dunes, maritime forests, and tidal marshes that provide critical habitat for both resident and migrating bird species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall migration and winter (September-February)
Best Times: Early morning in dunes and maritime forest
Unique Habitat: Rare coastal dune ecosystem with specialized species

Notable Species

Coastal Specialists: Seaside Sparrow, Saltmarsh Sparrow, Nelson's Sparrow
Raptors: Northern Harrier, Peregrine Falcon, Merlin
Maritime Forest: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers
Winter Visitors: Snow Bunting, Lapland Longspur (rare)
