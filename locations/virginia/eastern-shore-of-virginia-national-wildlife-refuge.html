About Eastern Shore of Virginia National Wildlife Refuge

Eastern Shore of Virginia National Wildlife Refuge is strategically located at the southern tip of the Delmarva Peninsula, creating one of the most important fall migration funnels on the East Coast. This 1,127-acre refuge concentrates enormous numbers of migrating hawks, songbirds, and butterflies as they prepare to cross the Chesapeake Bay. The refuge is world-renowned for its spectacular hawk migration and serves as a critical research site for bird migration studies.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall migration (September-October)
Best Times: Early morning for songbirds, midday for hawk migration
Hawk Platform: Excellent elevated viewing for raptor migration

Notable Species

Raptors: Peregrine Falcon, American Kestrel, Sharp-shinned Hawk, Cooper's Hawk
Migrants: Countless warblers, vireos, flycatchers during fall
Specialties: Migration research and banding demonstrations
Other: Monarch butterfly migration coincides with bird migration
