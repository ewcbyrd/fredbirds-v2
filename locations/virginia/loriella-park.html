About Loriella Park

Loriella Park is a hidden gem in Stafford County, featuring 40 acres of diverse habitats including wooded trails, a scenic pond, and varied edge environments. This suburban park offers excellent birding opportunities with its mix of mature forest, open water, and transitional habitats that attract both resident and migrating species. The park's accessibility and variety make it an ideal location for birders seeking a quick local outing with good diversity.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning around the pond and wooded trails
Accessibility: Well-maintained trails suitable for all levels

Notable Species

Waterbirds: Wood Duck, Great Blue Heron, Green Heron, Belted Kingfisher
Woodland Species: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers
Edge Habitat: Eastern Bluebird, Indigo Bunting, various sparrows
Year-round: Cardinals, chickadees, nuthatches, woodpeckers
