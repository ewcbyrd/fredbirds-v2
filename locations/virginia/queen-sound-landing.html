About Queen Sound Landing

Queen Sound Landing provides exceptional access to the bayside waters of Virginia's Eastern Shore, featuring extensive tidal flats and shallow waters that create ideal shorebird habitat. This location offers excellent opportunities to observe both resident and migrating shorebirds, wading birds, and waterfowl in a pristine Chesapeake Bay setting. The combination of tidal flats, marsh edges, and open water provides diverse feeding and resting areas for a wide variety of species throughout the year.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and fall migration (April-May, August-October)
Best Times: Early morning and changing tides for optimal shorebird activity
Tidal Flats: Extensive mudflats exposed at low tide

Notable Species

Shorebirds: Dunlin, Sanderling, Least Sandpiper, Ruddy Turnstone
Waterbirds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Snowy Egret
Terns and Gulls: Various species including Forster's Tern, Laughing Gull
Raptors: Osprey, Northern Harrier, Peregrine Falcon

Location

Queen Sound Landing
Accomack County, Virginia
Eastern Shore of Virginia

Access: Public boat landing with parking and bayside access
