About Highland Wildlife Management Area

Highland Wildlife Management Area encompasses over 17,000 acres of high elevation forests and mountain meadows in Virginia's Appalachian highlands. At elevations ranging from 2,000 to over 4,000 feet, this area provides habitat for montane bird species rarely found elsewhere in Virginia. The diverse Appalachian habitats include northern hardwood forests, spruce-fir stands, and mountain meadows that support a unique community of breeding and resident bird species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer breeding season (May-July)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum bird activity
Elevation: 2,000-4,000+ feet - dress for cooler mountain conditions

Notable Species

Mountain Specialties: Chestnut-sided Warbler, Rose-breasted Grosbeak, Winter Wren
High Elevation: Dark-eyed Junco, Golden-crowned Kinglet, Red Crossbill
Forest Birds: Various mountain warblers, vireos, thrushes
Game Birds: Wild Turkey, Ruffed Grouse
