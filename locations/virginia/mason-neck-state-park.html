About Mason Neck State Park

Mason Neck State Park encompasses 1,825 acres on a peninsula formed by the Potomac River, Belmont Bay, and Occoquan River. This park is famous for its role in Bald Eagle recovery and continues to be one of the most reliable places in Virginia to observe these magnificent raptors. The park features mature forests, marshes, and extensive Potomac River frontage, creating ideal habitat for both resident and migrating birds. The combination of diverse habitats and protected status makes it a premier birding destination year-round.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Year-round, especially winter for eagle viewing
Best Times: Early morning along shoreline and forest trails
Eagle Viewing: December through February for peak numbers

Notable Species

Signature Species: Bald Eagle (year-round resident population)
Raptors: Osprey, Red-tailed Hawk, Cooper's Hawk
Waterbirds: Great Blue Heron, Green Heron, various ducks
Forest Birds: Pileated Woodpecker, various warblers, vireos
