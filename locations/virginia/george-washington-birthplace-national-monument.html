About George Washington Birthplace National Monument

George Washington Birthplace National Monument encompasses 550 acres along the Potomac River, combining significant American history with excellent birding opportunities. The monument features diverse habitats including Potomac River frontage, historic grounds, open fields, and mature woodlands. This location provides a unique opportunity to observe birds while exploring the landscape where America's first president was born, offering both natural and cultural enrichment.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning along river and field edges
Historic Context: Birding among 18th-century colonial landscape

Notable Species

Raptors: Bald Eagle, Osprey, Red-tailed Hawk
Waterbirds: Great Blue Heron, various ducks, Belted Kingfisher
Woodland Species: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers
Field Birds: Eastern Bluebird, Indigo Bunting, various sparrows
