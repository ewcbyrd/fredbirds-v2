About Willis Wharf

Willis Wharf is an authentic working fishing village on Virginia's Eastern Shore, offering excellent birding opportunities in an active maritime setting. This picturesque location features fishing boats, tidal creeks, and coastal marshes that attract a diverse array of waterbirds and coastal species. The combination of active fishing operations and natural tidal habitats creates an ideal environment for observing both common and uncommon coastal birds, particularly those species that benefit from the fishing industry's activities.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Year-round, especially summer for breeding terns
Best Times: Early morning and when fishing boats return
Active Harbor: Working fishing village with constant bird activity

Notable Species

Coastal Birds: Brown Pelican, various gulls, terns
Fishing Associates: Osprey, Great Blue Heron, Great Egret
Harbor Specialists: Double-crested Cormorant, Laughing Gull
Tidal Creek: Various ducks, grebes, marsh birds

Address

Willis Wharf
Northampton County, Virginia
Eastern Shore of Virginia

Note: Active working harbor - observe from public areas and respect fishing operations
