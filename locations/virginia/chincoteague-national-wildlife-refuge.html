About Chincoteague National Wildlife Refuge

Chincoteague National Wildlife Refuge is one of the premier birding destinations on the East Coast, encompassing 14,000 acres of barrier island habitats including beaches, dunes, marshes, and maritime forests. Located on Assateague Island, this refuge is famous for its spectacular waterfowl concentrations, diverse shorebird populations, and the annual Peregrine Falcon migration. The refuge serves as a critical stopover point along the Atlantic Flyway and provides excellent birding opportunities year-round.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall migration and winter waterfowl (September-February)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Wildlife Drive: 3.2-mile auto tour provides excellent access to diverse habitats

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Snow Goose, Tundra Swan, various ducks, Canada Goose
Shorebirds: Piping Plover, American Oystercatcher, various sandpipers
Raptors: Peregrine Falcon, Northern Harrier, Merlin
Special: Wild ponies of Assateague Island
