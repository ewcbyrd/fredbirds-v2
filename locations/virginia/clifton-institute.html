About Clifton Institute

The Clifton Institute is a 900-acre environmental education center featuring diverse habitats including meadows, forests, streams, and ponds. This private non-profit organization provides excellent opportunities for observing woodland edge species, grassland birds, and wetland wildlife. The institute's varied ecosystems and commitment to habitat management create ideal conditions for both resident and migrating bird species throughout the year.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum bird activity
Trail Access: Multiple trails through diverse habitats

Notable Species

Grassland Birds: Eastern Bluebird, Indigo Bunting, Field Sparrow
Woodland Species: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers
Wetland Birds: Wood Duck, Great Blue Heron, Belted Kingfisher
Migrants: Spring and fall migrant songbirds
