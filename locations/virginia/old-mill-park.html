About Old Mill Park

Old Mill Park is a charming urban park situated along the Rappahannock River in historic Fredericksburg, featuring the ruins of an old mill and diverse riverine habitats. This accessible park combines historical significance with excellent birding opportunities, offering a mix of river access, wooded areas, and open spaces within the city limits. The park's location along the Rappahannock River corridor makes it an important stopover point for migrating birds and a year-round home for various resident species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and early summer (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning along river and wooded trails
Urban Setting: Accessible park within Fredericksburg city limits

Notable Species

Waterbirds: Belted Kingfisher, Great Blue Heron, Green Heron
Waterfowl: Wood Duck, various ducks, Pied-billed Grebe
Woodland Species: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers
Urban Adapters: Northern Mockingbird, Blue Jay, American Robin
