About Blandy Experimental Farm

Blandy Experimental Farm is a 700-acre University of Virginia research facility and arboretum featuring an impressive collection of over 5,000 trees and shrubs representing 800 species. The diverse habitats include open fields, woodland edges, mature forests, and ornamental plantings that attract a wide variety of bird species throughout the year. This location is particularly excellent for observing the relationship between different tree species and their associated bird communities.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum songbird activity
Special Features: Diverse tree species attract varied bird communities

Notable Species

Woodland Edge: Eastern Bluebird, Indigo Bunting, various sparrows
Migrants: Various warblers, vireos, flycatchers
Woodpeckers: Pileated, Red-bellied, Downy, Hairy
Year-round: Cardinals, Blue Jays, Carolina Wrens, Tufted Titmice
