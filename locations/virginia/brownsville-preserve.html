About Brownsville Preserve

Brownsville Preserve is a 500-acre coastal preserve on Virginia's Eastern Shore that protects diverse coastal habitats including salt marshes, maritime forests, and tidal creeks. Managed by The Nature Conservancy, this preserve provides critical habitat for coastal bird species and serves as an important stopover site for migrating birds along the Atlantic Flyway. The preserve's location on the Delmarva Peninsula offers excellent opportunities for observing both resident coastal species and seasonal migrants.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and fall migration (April-May, September-October)
Best Times: Early morning and around tidal changes
Access: Nature trails through coastal habitats

Notable Species

Coastal Specialists: Saltmarsh Sparrow, Clapper Rail, Marsh Wren
Migrants: Various warblers, vireos, flycatchers
Raptors: Northern Harrier, Osprey, Peregrine Falcon
Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Snowy Egret
