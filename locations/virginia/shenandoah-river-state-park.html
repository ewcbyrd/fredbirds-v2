About Shenandoah River State Park

Shenandoah River State Park encompasses 1,619 acres along the scenic Shenandoah River, offering excellent birding opportunities in diverse riparian and upland habitats. The park features extensive river frontage, mature forests, meadows, and wetlands that support a rich variety of bird species throughout the year. The combination of river access, varied elevations, and diverse habitats makes this park a premier destination for observing both woodland and waterbird species in the beautiful Shenandoah Valley.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning along river and forest trails
River Access: Multiple access points for waterbird observation

Notable Species

Waterbirds: Belted Kingfisher, Great Blue Heron, Green Heron
Raptors: Bald Eagle, Osprey, Red-tailed Hawk
Woodland Species: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers
River Specialists: Bank Swallow, Rough-winged Swallow, Wood Duck
