About Briery Branch Gap

Briery Branch Gap is a high elevation mountain gap in Rockingham County that provides access to excellent montane forest birding in the George Washington National Forest. At approximately 3,000 feet elevation, this location features diverse forest types from oak-hickory associations to higher elevation spruce and hemlock stands. The gap serves as an excellent location for observing mountain-dwelling bird species that are uncommon or absent at lower elevations.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer breeding season (May-July)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum bird activity
Elevation: Approximately 3,000 feet - bring layers for cooler temperatures

Notable Species

Mountain Specialties: Dark-eyed Junco, Winter Wren, Golden-crowned Kinglet
Mountain Warblers: Black-throated Blue, Black-throated Green, Canada Warbler
Other: Ruffed Grouse, Hermit Thrush, Red Crossbill (irregular)
Raptors: Broad-winged Hawk, Sharp-shinned Hawk

Location

Briery Branch Gap
George Washington National Forest
Rockingham County, Virginia
(Access via Forest Service roads)

Note: Remote mountain location - best accessed with local knowledge and 4WD recommended
