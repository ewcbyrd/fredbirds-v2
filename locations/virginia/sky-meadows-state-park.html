About Sky Meadows State Park

Sky Meadows State Park preserves 1,862 acres of historic farmland and open meadows in the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains. This scenic park features rolling meadows, woodland edges, and historic farm buildings that create ideal habitat for grassland and edge species. The park's diverse habitats and panoramic views make it an excellent destination for observing both resident and migrating birds, particularly species that prefer open grassland and agricultural environments.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and summer breeding season (April-July)
Best Times: Early morning in meadows and woodland edges
Grassland Habitat: Extensive open meadows ideal for grassland species

Notable Species

Grassland Specialists: Eastern Bluebird, Bobolink, Grasshopper Sparrow
Edge Species: Indigo Bunting, Field Sparrow, Blue-winged Warbler
Raptors: Red-tailed Hawk, American Kestrel, Northern Harrier
Historic Farm: Barn Swallow, Eastern Phoebe, Tree Swallow
