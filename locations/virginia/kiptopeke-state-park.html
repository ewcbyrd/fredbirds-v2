About Kiptopeke State Park

Kiptopeke State Park is world-renowned for its spectacular fall hawk migration and monarch butterfly migration, positioned at a critical funnel point on the Delmarva Peninsula. The park's 562 acres include beaches, dunes, maritime forests, and the famous hawkwatch platform that records thousands of raptors each fall. The coinciding migration of monarch butterflies creates a unique natural spectacle, making Kiptopeke one of the most exciting birding destinations on the East Coast during fall migration.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall migration (September-October)
Best Times: Early morning to midday for hawk migration
Hawkwatch Platform: Dedicated observation area with excellent views

Notable Species

Raptors: Sharp-shinned Hawk, Cooper's Hawk, Peregrine Falcon, Merlin
Peak Migration: American Kestrel, Broad-winged Hawk
Coastal Birds: Various gulls, terns, shorebirds
Special: Monarch butterfly migration coincides with bird migration
