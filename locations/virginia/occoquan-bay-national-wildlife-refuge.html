About Occoquan Bay National Wildlife Refuge

Occoquan Bay National Wildlife Refuge protects 644 acres of critical wetland habitat along the Potomac River, providing essential sanctuary for waterfowl and other wetland species. The refuge features diverse habitats including tidal marshes, freshwater impoundments, and wooded uplands that support an impressive variety of bird species throughout the year. Established in 1998, this refuge has become a cornerstone for waterfowl conservation in the region and offers excellent birding opportunities via its well-maintained trail system.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall migration and winter waterfowl (October-February)
Best Times: Early morning along impoundments and marsh edges
Trail System: Multiple trails provide access to diverse habitats

Notable Species

Raptors: Bald Eagle, Osprey, Northern Harrier
Waterfowl: Various ducks, geese, Pied-billed Grebe
Waterbirds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Green Heron
Marsh Species: Marsh Wren, Red-winged Blackbird, Swamp Sparrow
