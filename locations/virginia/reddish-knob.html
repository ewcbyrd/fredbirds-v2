About Reddish Knob

Reddish Knob stands as one of Virginia's highest accessible peaks at 4,397 feet elevation, offering spectacular mountain birding opportunities in mature montane forests. This scenic overlook provides access to high-elevation bird communities that are rare or absent at lower elevations, making it a premier destination for observing specialized mountain species. The combination of stunning panoramic views and unique bird life makes Reddish Knob an exceptional location for both birding and nature appreciation.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer breeding season (May-July)
Best Times: Early morning in montane forests and clearings
Elevation: 4,397 feet - Virginia's highest accessible peak

Notable Species

Mountain Specialties: Mourning Warbler, Canada Warbler, Winter Wren
High-Elevation: Common Raven, Red Crossbill, Golden-crowned Kinglet
Forest Birds: Various warblers, vireos, thrushes
Raptors: Broad-winged Hawk, Cooper's Hawk, Sharp-shinned Hawk
