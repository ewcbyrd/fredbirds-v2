About Grandview Nature Preserve

Grandview Nature Preserve is a 578-acre coastal preserve featuring 2.5 miles of pristine Chesapeake Bay beachfront, dunes, and maritime forest. This Hampton city preserve provides excellent opportunities for observing shorebirds, coastal migrants, and bay species throughout the year. The preserve's diverse coastal habitats and strategic location along the Chesapeake Bay make it an important stopover site for migrating birds and a year-round haven for resident coastal species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and fall migration (April-May, September-October)
Best Times: Early morning and around tidal changes
Beach Access: 2.5 miles of Chesapeake Bay beachfront

Notable Species

Shorebirds: Various sandpipers, plovers, yellowlegs, turnstones
Gulls & Terns: Laughing Gull, various terns, Ring-billed Gull
Migrants: Various warblers, vireos, flycatchers
Coastal Birds: Brown Pelican, Osprey, Great Blue Heron
