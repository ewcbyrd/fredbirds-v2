About Limberlost Trail

Limberlost Trail is a 1.3-mile accessible boardwalk trail through a beautiful hemlock forest in Shenandoah National Park. This unique trail provides excellent opportunities for observing mountain woodland birds while being accessible to birders of all mobility levels. The trail winds through a mature hemlock grove with spring wildflowers and diverse woodland bird species, making it one of the most accessible mountain birding experiences in Virginia.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and early summer (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum bird activity
Accessibility: Boardwalk trail suitable for all mobility levels

Notable Species

Mountain Specialties: Hermit Thrush, Veery, Winter Wren
Woodland Warblers: Black-throated Blue, Black-throated Green, Canada Warbler
Forest Birds: Barred Owl, Pileated Woodpecker, various vireos
Spring Highlights: Diverse warbler migration through hemlock forest
