About Huntley Meadows Park

Huntley Meadows Park is a 1,500-acre oasis featuring one of the largest freshwater marshes in the Washington D.C. metropolitan area. The park's extensive boardwalk system provides exceptional access to wetland birding, making it one of the premier locations in Northern Virginia for observing marsh birds, waterfowl, and secretive species. The diverse habitats include freshwater marsh, beaver ponds, meadows, and mature forests, supporting over 200 bird species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Boardwalk Access: Excellent elevated viewing over freshwater marsh

Notable Species

Marsh Specialties: King Rail, Least Bittern, Sora, Virginia Rail
Waterfowl: Wood Duck, Blue-winged Teal, various ducks
Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, Green Heron, Great Egret
Migrants: Various warblers, vireos, flycatchers
