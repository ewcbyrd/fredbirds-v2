About Straight Fork

Straight Fork represents one of Virginia's premier high-elevation birding destinations, featuring a pristine mountain stream valley in Highland County's Appalachian highlands. This remote location offers access to specialized mountain bird communities that thrive in the cool, moist conditions of high-elevation forests. The combination of rushing mountain streams, mature forest, and elevation gradients creates ideal habitat for species that are rare or absent at lower elevations, making it a must-visit destination for serious mountain birders.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer breeding season (May-July)
Best Times: Early morning along stream and forest trails
Mountain Stream: Pristine high-elevation aquatic habitat

Notable Species

Mountain Specialties: Canada Warbler, Blackburnian Warbler, Magnolia Warbler
Stream Associates: Winter Wren, Louisiana Waterthrush
High-Elevation: Dark-eyed Junco, Golden-crowned Kinglet
Forest Birds: Hermit Thrush, Veery, various vireos
