About Back Bay National Wildlife Refuge

Back Bay National Wildlife Refuge encompasses 9,250 acres of barrier island habitats including freshwater impoundments, maritime forests, and ocean beaches. Located on the Virginia Beach peninsula, this refuge provides critical habitat for wintering waterfowl and serves as an important stopover for migrating birds along the Atlantic Flyway. The refuge's diverse ecosystems support over 300 bird species, making it one of Virginia's premier birding destinations for both waterfowl and coastal species.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Winter waterfowl migration (December-February)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Access: Tram tours available, walking trails, wildlife drive

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Tundra Swan, Snow Goose, various ducks, Canada Goose
Specialty Birds: Loggerhead Shrike, Sedge Wren
Coastal Birds: Brown Pelican, various gulls and terns
Migrants: Various warblers, vireos, raptors during migration
