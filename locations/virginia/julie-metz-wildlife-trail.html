About Julie Metz Wildlife Trail

The Julie Metz Wildlife Trail is a accessible 0.6-mile boardwalk trail that winds through diverse wetland habitats in Woodbridge. This trail provides excellent opportunities for observing waterbirds, marsh species, and woodland edge birds in a compact, easily navigable setting. The elevated boardwalk allows for close observation of wetland wildlife while minimizing disturbance to sensitive habitats, making it an ideal location for birders of all mobility levels.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Boardwalk: 0.6-mile accessible trail through wetland habitats

Notable Species

Waterbirds: Great Blue Heron, Green Heron, Wood Duck
Wetland Species: Red-winged Blackbird, Marsh Wren, Common Yellowthroat
Woodland Edge: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers
Other: Belted Kingfisher, Tree Swallow, Eastern Bluebird
