About Margaret O'Brien Home

The Margaret O'Brien Home is situated in the heart of Highland County's mountain farmland, offering exceptional birding opportunities in a high-elevation Appalachian setting. This private location provides access to diverse mountain habitats including mixed forests, meadows, and farm edges that support a rich variety of breeding birds. The elevation and geographic location make it an excellent site for observing both common and specialized mountain species, including several that are rare or absent at lower elevations.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer breeding season (May-July)
Best Times: Early morning in meadows and forest edges
Elevation: High mountain setting with diverse Appalachian habitats

Notable Species

Mountain Specialties: Golden-winged Warbler, Chestnut-sided Warbler, Rose-breasted Grosbeak
Forest Birds: Various mountain warblers, vireos, Scarlet Tanager
Upland Game: Wild Turkey, Ruffed Grouse
Raptors: Broad-winged Hawk, Cooper's Hawk, Red-tailed Hawk
