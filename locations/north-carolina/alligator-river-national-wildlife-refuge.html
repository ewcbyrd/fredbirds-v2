About Alligator River National Wildlife Refuge

Alligator River National Wildlife Refuge encompasses over 154,000 acres of unique pocosin wetlands, providing critical habitat for an exceptional diversity of wildlife. This refuge is famous for its Red-cockaded Woodpecker population and serves as home to American black bears and the reintroduced red wolves. The diverse habitats include pocosins, pine forests, agricultural fields, and wetlands, creating excellent opportunities for observing both common and rare bird species in this pristine coastal plain ecosystem.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring and early summer breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning for maximum bird activity
Special Features: Red-cockaded Woodpecker viewing areas

Notable Species

Specialty Birds: Red-cockaded Woodpecker, Prothonotary Warbler
Raptors: Various hawks, Barred Owl, Great Horned Owl
Waterfowl: Wood Duck, various ducks in winter
Other Wildlife: American black bear, red wolf, American alligator
