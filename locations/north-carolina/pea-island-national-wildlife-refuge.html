About Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge

Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge occupies the northern portion of Hatteras Island and is renowned as one of the premier birding destinations on the East Coast. The refuge encompasses 5,834 acres of barrier island habitats including freshwater impoundments, salt marshes, and ocean beaches. The excellent boardwalk and observation areas provide outstanding access for viewing waterfowl, shorebirds, and wading birds, while the location serves as a critical stopover point along the Atlantic Flyway migration route.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Winter waterfowl season (November-February)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Boardwalk Access: Excellent viewing platforms over impoundments

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Tundra Swan, Snow Goose, various ducks, Canada Goose
Shorebirds: Various sandpipers, plovers, yellowlegs
Secretive Birds: American Bittern, rails, marsh birds
Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, various egrets, ibis
