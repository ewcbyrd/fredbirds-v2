About Bodie Island Lighthouse & Pond

The Bodie Island Lighthouse area combines historic charm with excellent birding opportunities, featuring the iconic 1872 lighthouse and surrounding freshwater pond system. This location provides a unique mix of coastal and wetland habitats within Cape Hatteras National Seashore, making it an ideal spot for observing both waterfowl and woodland species. The freshwater ponds attract a variety of birds year-round, while the surrounding marshes and maritime forest add to the diversity of species found here.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Spring migration and breeding season (April-June)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Historic Feature: Climb the lighthouse for panoramic views (seasonal)

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Wood Duck, Blue-winged Teal, various ducks
Wading Birds: Great Blue Heron, Great Egret, Green Heron
Woodland Birds: Various warblers, vireos, woodpeckers
Other: Red-winged Blackbird, Tree Swallow, Belted Kingfisher
