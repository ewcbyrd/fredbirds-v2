About Oregon Inlet Fishing Center

Oregon Inlet Fishing Center is a bustling hub of fishing activity that creates exceptional opportunities for observing gulls, terns, and other seabirds attracted to the fishing boats and their activities. Located at one of the most important inlets along the Outer Banks, this location provides access to both inlet waters and nearby ocean environments. The constant activity of charter boats and their fish cleaning operations attracts large numbers of seabirds, making it an excellent spot for observing feeding behaviors and gull identification challenges.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Year-round, especially during fishing season
Best Times: When fishing boats are returning with catches
Activity Peak: Late afternoon when boats return from offshore trips

Notable Species

Gulls: Laughing Gull, Ring-billed Gull, Herring Gull, Great Black-backed Gull
Terns: Royal Tern, Sandwich Tern, Forster's Tern
Other Seabirds: Brown Pelican, Double-crested Cormorant
Occasional: Rare gulls and terns attracted to fishing activity
