About Mattamuskeet National Wildlife Refuge

Mattamuskeet National Wildlife Refuge centers around Lake Mattamuskeet, North Carolina's largest natural lake, covering 40,000 acres of shallow freshwater habitat. This refuge is renowned for hosting one of the most spectacular waterfowl concentrations on the East Coast, with peak populations reaching over 100,000 birds during winter months. The refuge's extensive wildlife drives and observation areas provide excellent access to observe massive flocks of Tundra Swans, Snow Geese, and numerous duck species in this pristine coastal plain ecosystem.

Best Birding Times

Peak Season: Fall and winter waterfowl migration (November-February)
Best Times: Early morning and late afternoon
Wildlife Drive: Extensive road system for vehicle-based birding

Notable Species

Waterfowl: Tundra Swan, Snow Goose, Canada Goose, various ducks
Raptors: Bald Eagle, Northern Harrier, various hawks
Other: Great Blue Heron, American White Pelican (occasional)
Peak Numbers: Over 100,000 waterfowl during winter
