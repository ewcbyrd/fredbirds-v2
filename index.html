Welcome to Our Community

The Fredericksburg Birding Club has been bringing together bird enthusiasts from the Rappahannock River region since 2009. Whether you're a seasoned ornithologist or just beginning to discover the joy of birdwatching, our welcoming community offers opportunities to learn, explore, and share in the wonder of Virginia's diverse bird life.

Why Join Our Club?

Connect with fellow bird lovers, participate in guided field trips to premier birding locations, and contribute to citizen science projects that help protect our feathered friends.

Premier Birding Locations

The Fredericksburg area offers incredible diversity for birders, from riverine habitats to mature forests and suburban parks.

üåä Rappahannock River Trail

85+ species

Location: Fredericksburg, VA

Best for waterfowl, wading birds, and raptors. Peak times: early morning and late afternoon. Excellent for Great Blue Herons, Belted Kingfishers, and seasonal migrants.

üå≤ Motts Run Reservoir

120+ species

Location: Spotsylvania County, VA

Premier location for waterfowl and woodland species. Trail system offers diverse habitats. Notable for Wood Ducks, various warblers, and winter ducks.

üåä Aquia Landing Park

75+ species

Location: Stafford County, VA

Waterfront park along Aquia Creek offering excellent opportunities for waterfowl, waders, and migrating songbirds. Trail system winds through diverse habitats from creek edge to wooded areas.

üå≥ Chancellorsville Battlefield

95+ species

Location: Spotsylvania County, VA

Mix of forest edge and open areas. Excellent for cavity nesters, including various woodpecker species and Eastern Bluebirds.

üèûÔ∏è Crow's Nest Natural Area

110+ species

Location: Stafford County, VA

Diverse habitats from river access to mature forest. Notable for neotropical migrants, resident forest birds, and seasonal waterfowl.

üåø Caledon State Park

80+ species

Location: King George County, VA

Scenic park along the Potomac River featuring mature forests and river access. Notable for bald eagles, various woodpeckers, and seasonal migrants. Excellent trails through diverse habitats.
